What You Need to Know About Spas

A spa is a place where mineral-rich spring or seawater is used for healing baths. Spa resorts offer various treatments for relaxation and health. These practices date back to ancient times and remain popular today, especially in Europe and Japan.

Origins of the Term

The word “spa” comes from Spa, Belgium, known since Roman times as Aquae Spadanae. Some link it to the Latin word spargere (“to sprinkle”). Others believe it refers to the healing springs discovered there in the 14th century.

Treatments

Spa treatments are non-medical procedures that promote health and relaxation. They are commonly offered in resorts, day spas, and beauty salons.

Recent Trends

Today, spas combine beauty, wellness, and relaxation. The industry continues to expand globally, emphasizing overall well-being.

Types of Treatments

  • Day Spa — focuses on personal care and beauty
  • Destination Spa — wellness-focused resort experience
  • Spa Town — known for its natural healing waters
  • Foot Spa — simple, soothing treatment for the feet
